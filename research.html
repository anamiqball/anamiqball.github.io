Research interests

Distributed systems; storage and caching; tiered memory; key-value (KV) stores; measurement and observability; fault tolerance.

Systems and Storage Group — University of Utah (Prof. Ryan Stutsman)

Aug 2025 – Present • ObjecTier and Segcache: measurement-driven memory tiering and hot-object consolidation
  • Extended Segcache with a stable scan interface for background analysis while preserving time-to-live (TTL) expiration behavior.
  • Added epoch-scoped hit counters to estimate recency-weighted hotness, then aggregated hotness at both segment level and 4 kibibyte (KiB) page level.
  • Built a Python trace-replay and analysis pipeline to summarize skew with distributional views (for example, cumulative distribution functions (CDFs)) and guide placement decisions.
  • Benchmarked dynamic random-access memory (DRAM) / solid-state drive (SSD) tiering against least recently used (LRU) / least frequently used (LFU) baselines on skew-shift traces; ran sensitivity and ablation studies.

FLUX Research Group — University of Utah (Prof. Jacobus Van der Merwe)

Dec 2025 – Present • Measurement workflows for open 5G stacks and mobility behavior
  • Collected protocol traces and packet captures in controlled experiments to tie user-visible disruption to concrete mobility events.
  • Built repeatable analysis workflows that connect hypotheses to measurements, with an emphasis on observability and validation.
